Black-grass (Alopecurus myosuroides) is an annual grass species belonging to the Poaceae family. It is a problematic weed in agricultural fields, particularly in Europe. The plant has slender, green leaves with a distinctive boat-shaped tip and produces dense, cylindrical flower spikes. The flower spikes turn purplish-black as they mature, giving the plant its common name. Black-grass competes with crops for resources and can significantly reduce crop yields if not effectively managed.

Charlock (Rhamphospermum arvense) is an annual flowering plant belonging to the Brassicaceae family. It is commonly found in agricultural fields and along roadsides. Charlock has bright yellow flowers with four petals and lance-shaped leaves that are often hairy. It is considered a weed and can be invasive in some regions, competing with crops for nutrients and space. Charlock is known for its ability to tolerate a wide range of soil conditions.

Cleaver (Galium aparine) is an herbaceous annual plant belonging to the Rubiaceae family. Cleaver is characterized by its clinging nature, with small hooked hairs that enable it to attach to surfaces and climb on surrounding vegetation. The plant produces small, white, star-shaped flowers that develop into small, spherical fruits covered in bristles. Cleaver is commonly found around the world in damp, shady areas. Cleaver has been used in traditional medicine for its potential diuretic and detoxifying benefits.

Common Chickweed (Stellaria media) is a small, flowering annual plant belonging to the Caryophyllaceae family. It is an herbaceous weed commonly found in temperate regions around the world. It is known for its adaptability, thriving in various environments such as lawns and disturbed areas. It has been used in traditional medicine for its potential health benefits.
